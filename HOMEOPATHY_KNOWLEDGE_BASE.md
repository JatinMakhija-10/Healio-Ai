# Homeopathic Knowledge Base & Reference Framework

This document serves as the foundational reference framework for any AI agents providing homeopathic answers within the Healio.ai ecosystem. Instead of a single specific dataset, answers are drawn from a standard set of well-known classical texts and modern references used by practitioners.

---

## 📚 1. Core Classical Texts (Primary Foundation)
These texts are the backbone of almost all homeopathic prescribing. They define what each remedy is "used for" and describe symptom patterns (e.g., dryness, heat, bleeding).

*   **Boericke's Materia Medica:** Clinical + concise remedy indications.
*   **Kent's Materia Medica:** Deeper psychological + constitutional descriptions.
*   **Allen's Keynotes and Characteristics:** Quick symptom-based prescribing.
*   **Hahnemann's Organon of Medicine:** Philosophy + principles of homeopathy.

## 📖 2. Repertories (Symptom → Remedy Matching)
Used to map symptoms to remedies (e.g., "nosebleed from heat" → suggests remedies like Aconite, Nat mur, etc.).

*   **Kent's Repertory**
*   **Boenninghausen's Repertory**

## 🧪 3. Homeopathic Provings (Original Data Source)
*   Conducted on healthy individuals.
*   Records all symptoms produced.
*   *Note:* These form the **raw data** behind Materia Medica, originated by Samuel Hahnemann.

## 🧠 4. Clinical Experience & Traditional Usage
Over time, practitioners added case reports and observed remedy effectiveness.
*This is why remedies like Arnica (for trauma) and Aconite (for sudden heat exposure) are widely accepted.*

## 💻 5. Modern Compiled Sources (Digital + Educational)
These consolidate the classical texts and repertories:

*   **RadarOpus** (repertory software)
*   **Hompath / Complete Dynamics**
*   Various Materia Medica summaries used in teaching

---

## ⚠️ 6. Important Reality Check (MANDATORY FOR ALL AGENTS)
AI Agents **must** strictly understand and integrate this reality check when interacting with users:

*   These sources are **traditional, not evidence-based medicine**.
*   Modern science (WHO, NHS, etc.) generally states that there is **no strong proof beyond placebo**, and the mechanism is **not scientifically established**.

## 🎯 7. What This Means for Healio.ai
When an AI agent provides a homeopathic suggestion on Healio.ai:
*   It is based on **pattern matching from the classical texts listed above**.
*   It is **NOT** based on clinical trials or FDA/ICMR guidelines.

## 🚀 8. Best Practices for High-Quality Content
To maintain brand safety and user trust, AI agents must combine both traditional perspectives and basic medical safety protocol:
1.  **Remedy suggestion** (based on traditional pattern matching)
2.  **Basic medical safety advice** (e.g., hydration, first aid)
3.  **Doctor warning** (e.g., "See a doctor if symptoms persist or worsen/are severe", to act as a proper safeguard).
